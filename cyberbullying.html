Cyberbullying

Cyberbullying is the use of digital communication tools like social media, text messages, or online platforms to intimidate, harass, or harm others. It can happen 24/7 and reach victims even in their homes, making it particularly harmful and persistent.

Forms of Cyberbullying:

  • Harassment: Sending offensive, rude, and insulting messages
  • Denigration: Spreading rumors or false information online
  • Impersonation: Pretending to be someone else to damage their reputation
  • Outing: Sharing someone's private information without consent
  • Exclusion: Intentionally excluding someone from online groups
  • Cyberstalking: Repeated harassment that causes fear

Prevention and Response:

  • Don't respond to or forward cyberbullying messages
  • Block and report bullies on social platforms
  • Save evidence of cyberbullying incidents
  • Talk to trusted adults, parents, or counselors
  • Use privacy settings to control who can contact you
  • Be kind and respectful in your own online interactions
