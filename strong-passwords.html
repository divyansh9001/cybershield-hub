Strong Passwords

A strong password is your first line of defense against unauthorized access to your accounts. It should be complex enough to resist guessing and brute-force attacks while being memorable enough for you to use effectively.

Characteristics of Strong Passwords:

  • Length: At least 12-16 characters long
  • Complexity: Mix of uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols
  • Uniqueness: Different password for each account
  • Unpredictability: Avoid personal information and common patterns
  • No Dictionary Words: Avoid common words that can be easily guessed

Password Creation Tips:

  • Use passphrases with random words: "Coffee!Mountain#Blue92"
  • Substitute characters: Replace 'a' with '@', 'o' with '0'
  • Use password managers to generate and store passwords
  • Avoid personal information like birthdays or names
  • Don't use the same password across multiple accounts
  • Update passwords regularly, especially after security breaches

Password Management:

  • Use reputable password managers (1Password, Bitwarden, LastPass)
  • Enable two-factor authentication where available
  • Never share passwords with others
  • Avoid writing passwords down in plain text
